Setup

Before the beginning of the game each player is dealt a card

Game play

Love Letter is played in a series of rounds. Each round represents one day. At the end of each round, one player's letter reaches Princess Annette, and she reads it.

When she reads enough letters from one suitor, she becomes enamored and grants that suitor permission to court her. That player wins the princess's heart and the game.

Taking a turn

On your turn, draw the top card from the deck and add it to your hand. Then choose one of the two cards in your hand and discard it face up in front of you. Apply any effect on the card you discarded. You must apply its effect, even if it is bad for you.

All discarded cards remain in front of the player who discarded them. Overlap the cards so that it's clear in which order they were discarded. This helps players to figure out which cards other players might be holding.

Out of the round

If a player is knocked out of the round, that player discards the card in his or her hand face up (do not apply the card's effect) and takes no more turns until next round.

End of a round

A round ends if the deck is empty at the end of a turn. All players still in the round reveal their hands. The player with the highest ranked person wins the round. In case of a tie, the player who discarded the highest total value of cards wins.
A round also ends if all players but one are out of the round, in which case the remaining player wins.
The winner receives a token of affection. All 16 cards are shuffled, and a new round begins, the winner of the previous round goes first.

End of game

The game ends when someone collects the necessary number affection tokens to win the game.

    This is:
  • for 2 players: 7 tokens
  • for 3 players: 5 tokens
  • for 4 players: 4 tokens

Cards


  • 1. Guard
    When you discard a Guard card, you have to choose a player and name a non-Guard card. If that player has that card, that player is knocked out of the round.
    If all the other players still in the round are protected by the Handmaid, then this card does nothing

  • 2.Priest
    When you discard a Priest card, you can look at one other player's hand.

  • 3. Baron
    When you discard the Baron, choose one other player still in the round. You and that player will compare your hands. The player with the lower rank is knocked out of the round. In case of a tie, nothing happens.
    If all other players still in the round are protected by the Handmaid, this card does nothing

  • 4.Handmaid
    When you discard the Handmaid, you are protected from the effects of other playersâ€™ cards until your next turn.

  • 5. Prince
    When you discard the card Prince, choose one player still in the round (including yourself). That player discards his or her hand (do not apply its effect) and draws a new card.
    If all other players are protected by the Handmaid, you must choose yourself.

  • 6. King
    When you discard the King, choose a player with whom you'll trade the card in your hand with the card held by that player. You cannot trade with a player who is out of the round, nor with someone protected by the Handmaid.
    If all other players still in the round are protected by the Handmaid, this card does nothing.

  • 7. Countess
    The countess' effect is different from the other cards'. It applies when it's still in your hand. If you ever have the Countess and either the King or Prince in your hand, you must discard the Countess.

  • 8. Princess
    If you discard the Princess, for whatever reason, you're out of the round.
